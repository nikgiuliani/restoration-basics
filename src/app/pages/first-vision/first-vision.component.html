First Vision Accounts

The First Vision is a pivotal event in the history of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. It was the moment when God the Father and His Son, Jesus Christ, appeared to Joseph Smith in answer to his prayer about which church to join. The First Vision represents the beginning of the restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ in its fullness, and it laid the foundation for the Church's beliefs.


According to the this article by the Joseph Smith Papers, there are four different accounts of the First Vision, recorded by Joseph Smith at different years in his life: 1832, 1835, 1838, 1842. The differences in accounts can be attributed to factors such as changes in Joseph Smith's perspective over time, the intended audience, and the purpose for which each account was given. Despite the slight variations in details, all accounts testify of the reality of the vision and the divine nature of the message Joseph received.
